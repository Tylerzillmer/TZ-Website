How does the population affect the energy consumtpion in the US?

For this project we decided to analyze three different states in the US, California, New Jersey, and Texas. Each state had its data set on tuva.
We decided to use these heat graphs as a way to visually incorporate three variables. The heat colors are also better for those looking at it to visualize the data

Data Visuals and descriptions

This graph is a visual of the energy consumption in NJ, you can see a common trend through out most of the graph,
the higher the population, the more energy consumption, this is mostly true except for the end where there was
more people but they decreased energy consumption.

This is a graph of the energy consumption in Texas. You can see a very distinct trend in the population,
year and energy consumption, it is very clear that over time the poulation increase which also creates more energy
consumption.

Creative Visuals

We can see in this graph the stats of 1985, similar to the previous graphs we have used color to visualize population.
In 1985 there was a fairly high poplation in Califronia and New Jersey which was represented by the lrager images, and they also had a high energy consumption rate.
As for Texas their population was lower so they used less energy.

Conclusion

After a lot of research and analysis we found that there is a consistent trend between the relationship of poulation and energy consumption,
as population and time increased the energy consumption also grew. They happened at the same rate.
We thought a lot about this and we think that the government should create ways to use the growing population to decrease thet energy consumption,
this will be very sustainable in the future.
